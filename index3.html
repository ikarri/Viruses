What Is MERS?

MERS is one of the viruses that have occured so far. It stands for Middle East Respiratory Syndrome, which is a viral respiratory illness. This virus was found in Saudi Arabia in September 2012. It is kind of similar to the CoronaVirus. The virus was first founded in some animals in the Arabian Peninsula, some people have found MERS-CoV in camels from many countries. Direct contact with camels and humans is dangerous and can have a high risk. The symptoms for MERS-CoV is having a fever, cough, and shortness of breath. It spreaded by close contact with people, coughing, and some of the people that traveled near the Arabian Peninsula have got infected. You can also get infected if you live with someone that has gotten infected. If you are sick, the symptoms don't immediately show, they show after a few days. People aged from 1-99 years have gotten infected for MERS-CoV.

What Is SARS?

SARS is also one of the viruses that has occured. The full form of SARS is Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome which is also a viral respiratory illness. SARS first cases were found in Southern China in November 2002. The virus spread to more than a dozen countries in North America, South America, Europe, and Asia. Some of the symptoms of SARS is a high fever, aches, respiratory symptoms, 10-20% of patients have diarrhea, some have dry cough, and pneumonia. After 2004, no new SARS cases were reported. It can spread by close contact, coughing, sneezing, and it is better not to touch your face.
